Research interests

My future research interests lie at the intersection of QFT and Gravity, with focus on physics beyond perturbation theory. Especially when it comes to quantum gauge theories there is a lot of non-perturbative aspects we yet do not fully understand. To only name a few we have the Gribov ambiguity, extended field configurations or the phenomena of Confinement. Lattice QFT appears to me to be a tool that might help us make severe progress in the understanding of these topics. But there also exists other non-perturvative tools like Dyson Schwinger Equations, the Functional Renormalization Group approach or Holography. While I would like to learn more about each of them, especially the last one, using the structural similarities between Gravity and QFT, is of great interest to me.
I also always had a fable for Quantum Field Theory in curved spacetime. Recently the algebraic apporach caught my attention. Most of the work done in this area however is of perturbative nature. In my opinion, algebraic quantum field theory potenially provides a framework to deal with physical issues, for example the notion of particles or antimatter in a curved spacetime background, while simultanious offering a mathmathical rigorous description of QFT. Of course with the caveat of beeing a semi-classical description, not taking into account quantum-gravity effects. The question if such semi-classical descriptions are valid at all in order to adress for example the information-loss-paradox of Black Holes remains an open question of research. One to whose answer I would be glad to contribute to in the near future.

My current research project

One of the most prominent problems of modern physics is the mystery of Dark matter. Despite ample observational evidence from astrophysics, there is yet no commonly accepted explanation on the level of particle physics. Several models already have been proposed among which so called WIMP dark matter has been investigated most intensively. In the early 2000 Spergel and Steinhardt proposed that sufficiently strong self interactions among dark matter particles might resolve some structure formation puzzles like the so called Cusp vs. Core problem. Recent works by
In my Masters thesis I investigate a class of models for composite dark matter based on a strongly interacting gauge model. In these szenarios dark matter is made up of bound states (dark pions) of some fermions (dark quarks). Due to the underlying new strong interaction sufficiently large self interaction between the dark matter particles are introduced. Futhermore the stability of the dark matter can be explained by an accidental flavor symmetry.
My model focuses on fermions that are charged under a real representation, leaving the specific choice of gauge group and representation an open parameter of the theory. As it turns out the reality condition is strong enough to qualitativly restrict all the low energy properties of the theory, while the qualitative features manifest in the low energy effective constants, dependent on the choice of gauge group and representation. The behavor differs from the complex representation of QCD and SU(N) theories and resembles more a SO(N)-like type of theory. The relevant particles my model describes are fermion-fermion bound states, called dark mesons. The lightest stable states are the pseudo-scalar PNGBs, called dark pions, of a spomtanous symmetry breaking szenario. Further the lightest vector meson states (rho, omega) and a pseudo-scalar flavor-singlet meson (eta) related to the chiral anomaly are included.
If one restricts to a model with 2 fermions one encounters a topological obstruction that leads to the breakdown of Witten's geometrical construction of the socalled "Anomalous" or "topological" terms in the effective field theory. We had to go back to the original approach of Wess and Zumino , which directly related the UV-properties with the IR-properties by investigation of anomalous Ward identities in order to construct these terms. These terms incorporate a 3->2 interaction of the pions which contributes (and even may domintate) the freez out Szenario of dark matter. This socalled SIMP mechanism was proposed and investigated by the group around Yonit Hochberg .
The model may be viewed as standard modell extension and can be coupled to the SM via a Portal mediator. We investigate the case of a massive dark-photon meditor, kinetically mixing with standard model hypercharge. After the introduction of the mediator dark matter annihilation into the standard model compete with the 3->2 process in the freezout. However another decay channel opens with anomalous vertex of the dark eta particle. At the last step of the project we investigate the freeze out szenario in view of these 3 channels.

Conferences

Composite dark matter from non-abelian gauge theories with real representations.
DSU 2022, Dark Side of the Universe Conference in Sydney [Parallel sessions talk]
We present a class of composite dark matter models with matter fields in a real representation under the non-abelian gauge group. As an exemplary theory we present a Sp(4) gauge theory, with fermions transforming in the antisymmetric representation. We analyze the structure of these theories in the UV and discuss the building blocks of the low energy effective theory in the IR, describing the dynamics of the cold dark matter. We put special focus on the construction of topological terms responsible for 3→2 cannibalization processes within the dark sector and on the physics of the pseudo scalar singlet related to the axial anomaly. We show that both these aspects significantly affect the freeze out calculation of the dark matter relic abundance and thus are relevant for investigations of the parameter space of such theories.

Low energy effective description of dark Sp(4) theory with matter in non-fundamental representation.
ÖPG 2022, The 71st Annual Meeting of the Austrian Physical Society in Leoben [Parallel sessions talk]
Dark matter makes up the majority of the mass in the universe. Despite ample observational evidence, dark matter has yet no explanation in particle physics. This drives construction of new particle physics theories which can lead to interesting explanations for the nature of dark matter. We present a new particle physics model where dark matter is a composite object much like pions in the Standard Model. Our model is based on a new gauge group -- Sp(4), with matter in antisymmetric representation rather than fundamental representation. We sketch the building blocks of this theory and explain how such theories are different than the usually encountered constructions in particle physics.

Theses

Analytische Berechnung der spontanen Magnetisierung von isotropen homogenen Ising Ferromagneten unter der Verwendung von Graßmann Zahlen
Bachelor thesis [In German]
Ziel dieser Arbeit ist die Angabe einer möglichst eleganten Herleitung für die exakte Formel der spontanen Magnetisierung des 2d Ising-Modells in Abhängigkeit von der Temperatur, welche erstmals von Lars Onsager aufgestellt wurde. Dies wird bewerkstelligt indem das graphisch-kombinatorische Problem, welches bei der Beschreibung der Zustandssumme und der Magnetisierung auftritt, mithilfe von Graßmann-Zahlen in ein handhabbares algebraisches Problem übersetzt wird. Mathematische Theoreme wie der starke Szegö Grenzwertsatz dienen zudem als praktische Werkzeuge um Grenzübergänge im Thermodynamischen Limes elegant zu behandeln. Die Arbeit beschränkt sich dabei auf ein homogenes isotropes zweidimensionales Ising-Gitter mit periodischen Randbedingungen. Die Ideen zu dieser Herleitung gehen auf die Arbeiten von Stuart Samuel, Elliot W.Montroll, Renfrey B. Potts und John C. Ward zurück.

Student projects

Particle creation in an expanding universe.
Student project for a course by Reinhard Alkofer
In this project we consider a real scalar quantum field coupled in an expanding spatially-flat FLRW universe. The case of minimal and conformal coupling to the background metric are considered and for the FLRW scaling factor we picked the non-physical choice of a Sauter pulse. Due to the lack of an appropriate notion of time-translation symmetry, energy conservation is violated and one typically expects particle creation. The problem is analytically solvable in terms of hypergeometric functions and thus allows a nice discussion of the rate and amount of produced particles in terms of their mass, the expansion rate and the total expansion.

A simple way to explain phenomena at the event horizon of a static black hole.
Student project for a course by Reinhard Alkofer
In this project we treat a thought-experiment of an astronaut near the event Horizon of a static Black Hole, who drops a Probe into the Black Hole and measures the light signal transmitted by the Probe. We review the properties of the Schwarzschild spacetime in different charts, explain the disadvantages and ambiguities of the Schwarzschild chart and introduce non-pathological coordinates such as lightcone coordinates and Kruskal-Szekeres coordinates. The redshifts, null-geodesics, horizion as well as the introduction of a proper time-orientation on the spacetime are discussed. The apparent paradox, that the probe drops into the black hole after finite time without the astronaut ever observing, it is neatly explained and visualized by the use of these new coordinates in a Penrose diagram.

Functional Renormalization Group approach for interaction Dirac fermions.
Student project for a course by Reinhard Alkofer
In this project we derive the fermionic version of the Wetterich equation for the functional renormalization group flow and apply it to the Gross-Neveu model with N fermion flavors. A simplistic ansatz for the quantum effective action allows for an analytic solution of the system. The flow equation and the running of couplings are calculated and discussed.
